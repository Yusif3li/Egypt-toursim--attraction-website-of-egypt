Sinai

This massive desert covers 23,000 square miles. Filled with natural and historical wonders like the biblical heritage site Mount Sinai, and dotted with resorts along the Red Sea, you will never run out of things to do in Sinai.

Cairo

Immerse yourself in the wonders of Cairo, Egypt’s glistening capital city. Steeped in history and surrounded by beautiful views, Cairo is the center of Egyptian life and culture, both ancient and modern. From the capital you’re just moments away from the Pyramids of Giza and the Nile River, and inside the city you can enjoy the bustling atmosphere of the Khan El Khalili Bazaar, see museums dedicated to Ancient Egypt, taste delicious treats, and much, much more. We’ve put together this guide to the absolute best travel experiences and tours of Cairo, Egypt.

Hurghada

Stunning coral reefs and turquoise waters perfect for windsurfing have made Hurghada, on Egypt's Red Sea Coast, a busy resort town. Within easy reach of the stunning Giftun Islands and the Eastern Arabian Desert, Hurghada has seen enormous amounts of development in the past decade—and yes, it does seem overrun with tourists at times. But it’s a relatively easy beach escape for Europeans, and some of the world's best diving and snorkeling sites are just offshore. Walk or catch a cab to explore the old quarter, El Dahar.

Luxor

Luxor, the beautiful city in Southern Egypt, is home to Egypt’s greatest hidden gem: the Ancient Egyptian city of Thebes. Known as the world’s greatest open-air museum, Luxor is teeming with historical landmarks, gorgeous vistas, and secrets of the ancient world. Visit Luxor to have a truly unique adventure in Egypt.
